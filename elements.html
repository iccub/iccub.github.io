IDN Spoofing Awareness Demo

Training purpose only: Internationalized Domain Names can contain Unicode characters that look similar to ordinary Latin letters. Attackers may abuse this to create confusing links.

Example: Lookalike Characters

The following two strings may look similar at a glance:

Normal Latin text: example.test

Mixed-script lookalike text: exаmple.test

In the second version, the letter that looks like a may be a Cyrillic character instead of a Latin one.

Safe Link Inspection Practice

Hover over this harmless local-only training link and inspect the browser’s status bar:

Training link: example.test

Real security lesson: do not rely only on what link text says. Check the actual destination, certificate details, password manager behavior, and whether the site is expected.

Defensive Checks

  • Watch for mixed scripts, such as Latin plus Cyrillic characters.
  • Use password managers; they usually match exact domains.
  • Check the real URL before entering credentials.
  • Consider browser or email gateway protections that flag IDN homographs.
  • Convert suspicious Unicode domains to Punycode before trusting them.
